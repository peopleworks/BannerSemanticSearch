BC029|Payroll Tax|SUI — State Unemployment Insurance Terminology|PHRDEDN,PWVEARN|Multiple|SUI = State Unemployment Insurance, the state-level payroll tax that funds unemployment benefits for laid-off workers. Three distinct terms that often get confused. (1) SUI tax: what the employer pays to the state fund — in most states employees pay nothing. (2) SUI subject wages: the gross wages eligible for SUI, before the annual per-employee cap is applied — this is the reporting figure. (3) SUI taxable wages: the portion after the cap is applied — only this amount is actually taxed. Annual wage caps vary by state: Illinois $13,271, Michigan $9,500, New York $12,500 (2025 values). Example: an Illinois employee earning $50,000 has subject wages of $50,000 and taxable wages of $13,271 (capped). Cap logic: ytd_taxable = LEAST(ytd_subject, state_cap); qtd_taxable = MAX(ytd_taxable - prior_ytd_taxable, 0). FUTA (Federal Unemployment Tax Act) is the federal counterpart; government entities including public community colleges are FUTA-exempt under IRC 3306(c)(7).|ytd_taxable = LEAST(ytd_subject, state_cap); qtd_taxable = MAX(ytd_taxable - prior_ytd_taxable, 0)|ADP SmartCompliance Integration
